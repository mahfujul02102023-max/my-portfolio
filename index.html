MD MAHFUJUL ISLAM

Seeking Summer Internship / Part-time position in Environmental Science

Interdisciplinary PhD student in Environmental Science (Department of Natural Resources and Society) at the University of Idaho, funded by the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) in collaboration with Idaho National Laboratory (INL). My doctoral research focuses on maximizing fermentable sugar yield from lignocellulosic biomass by blending municipal solid waste (MSW) with agricultural residues, evaluating how different pretreatment methods and blend ratios affect cellulose accessibility during enzymatic hydrolysis to identify cost-effective combinations for scalable waste-to-bioenergy conversion. With a strong foundation in Physics and 8+ years of university-level teaching and research experience, I have published work in environmental regulatory policy, materials science, and sustainability.

My hands-on research experience spans Bangladesh, France, and the USA, bringing practical skills in comparative policy analysis, quantitative and qualitative data interpretation, experimental design, and scientific writing. I have a track record of designing inclusive curricula, mentoring students across disciplinary boundaries, and facilitating critical discussions in diverse academic settings. I am actively looking for opportunities in environmental science, environmental consulting, sustainability, or related fields where I can apply my analytical and research skills to real-world environmental and energy challenges.
